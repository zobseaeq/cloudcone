## What Is CloudCone?

CloudCone is a US-based cloud hosting company that's been running since around 2014 (operating under the Quadcone umbrella since 2012). They run KVM-based VPS servers and a premium tier called SC2 (Scalable Cloud Compute) out of three US data centers: Los Angeles, St. Louis (Missouri, opened September 2025), and Reston (Virginia, opened October 2025).

Their whole thing is simple: real dedicated VPS resources on Intel® Xeon® processors and enterprise-grade Dell hardware, at prices that make other providers look like they're charging Manhattan rent. All data centers carry SOC II, HIPAA, and PCI certifications. DDoS protection powered by Voxility is included for free with every server.

They're not trying to compete with DigitalOcean or AWS. They know what they are — a budget-first provider for people who know their way around a Linux terminal.

## VPS vs. SC2 — Which One Do You Want?

Here's the honest breakdown:

**Go with VPS if:** you want the lowest possible price, you're comfortable managing your own backups, and the workload isn't mission-critical. Developers running staging environments, personal sites, bots, media servers — this is your lane.

**Go with SC2 if:** you want automated backups baked in, a feature-rich dashboard, one-click application deployment, scalable resources, and prioritized support. The SC2 Hashtag deals make this tier accessible at prices that used to only buy budget VPS.

The key difference that actually matters day-to-day: **SC2 has automated backups and snapshots built in. Budget VPS does not.** Keep that in mind.
